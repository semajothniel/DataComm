Summary of What We Did This Semester in Data Communication

This semester in Data Communication, we studied how computers and electronic devices communicate with each other through networks. We learned the importance of transferring data accurately, quickly, and securely across communication channels.

The course introduced us to the basic components of data communication systems, including the sender, receiver, transmission medium, message, and communication protocols. We also explored how different communication devices interact within a network environment.

  • Introduction to Data Communication: Understanding the meaning and importance of communication systems.
  • Components of Data Communication: Learning about transmitters, receivers, signals, and channels.
  • Transmission Media: Studying guided and unguided media such as fiber optics, twisted pair cables, and wireless communication.
  • Network Topologies: Exploring star, ring, bus, mesh, and tree topologies.
  • Types of Networks: Understanding LAN, MAN, WAN, and their practical applications.
  • OSI Reference Model: Learning the seven layers of network communication and their functions.
  • Communication Protocols: Studying protocols such as TCP/IP and how devices follow rules during communication.
  • Error Detection and Correction: Understanding methods used to ensure reliable data transmission.
  • Wireless Communication: Learning how mobile phones, Wi‑Fi, and satellites communicate.
  • Internet and Networking Basics: Exploring how the internet operates and how devices connect globally.

Through this course, we gained knowledge about modern communication technologies and how networks are designed to improve communication in businesses, schools, and everyday life.
